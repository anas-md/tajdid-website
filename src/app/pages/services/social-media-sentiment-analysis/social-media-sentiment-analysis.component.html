Social Media Sentiment Analytics

Sentiment analysis is created by using approaches from data science and artificial intelligence (AI) technology to deliver responses to people's opinions on specific commercial products and services. Sentiment analytics seeks to analyse and comprehend emotional sentiments and emotions conveyed through social media, consumer feedback, or customer surveys.

Sentiment analytics is mostly used for customer experience management, reputation management, brand management, market research, and competitive analysis.

How does it work ?

This Sentiment Analytics works with data from social media to analyse and understand (insights).

Data will be collected from social media and analysed automatically by the system.

Customers' and the general public's thoughts, discoveries, feelings, and suggestions will be compiled into insight reports.

Features

Sentiment

A visual display will demonstrate the public's attitude towards a product or service, whether positive, negative, or neutral.

Emotion

The emotional visual display displays people's feelings towards a service or product, whether they are happy, furious, or sad.

Recommendations

A sentiment analytics visual display shows people's recommendations based on data collecting.

Benefits

  • Empowering organisations to understand client needs and sentiments, maintain competition, and make data-driven decisions.
  • Give the business an understanding (insights) about the business position, then help in making more accurate business decisions.
  • Manage business reputation by using sentiment and emotion surveys offered by customers on social media.
  • Analysis of business competitiveness through direct comparison of data and feedback offered by own and competitor consumers.
  • Determine the extent to which customer perceptions connect to brand and product strengths and aid in the development of effective marketing initiatives.
  • Help organisations track and manage unanticipated crises through customer reactions, as well as knowing the extent to which customers have moved on to other issues after the crisis in a particular length of time.
